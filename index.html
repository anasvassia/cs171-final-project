A Cover Worth 1000 Words


how aesthetic features can influence your decisions
The World of Aesthetics


Aesthetics tend to be taken for granted. Who cares if something looks good? Function before style, right? While there is some truth to that, visual appeal definitely plays an important role in our everyday lives. Aesthetics can affect everything from how we feel, to the decisions that we make - sometimes without us even realizing it!

Often when we think ‘aesthetics’, the first things that come to mind are probably envy-enticing photos that flood Instagram and Tumblr. However, aesthetic is used in the creation of books, movies, and even music, and is an overarching term that includes everything from typography to delivery. Arguably, it’s these more basic building blocks that can have the biggest influence on the human psyche.

Whether you’re a customer or a product designer, learning more about aesthetics can really help you to make savvier decisions. Not convinced? Understandable - it can be a tough concept to wrap your head around, and your first instinct might be to click out of this page entirely. Let’s see if we can change your mind though.

We’ve used data from zygmuntz’s goodbooks dataset (a compilation of data - genre, ratings, etc.- of various books) from the book-rating site, goodreads) as well as information gathered by running book cover images through Google’s Vision API (using their “scores” result) to figure out the dominant colors and symbols used on different book covers) in order to create the informative visualizations on this website. Hopefully they can help to provide some insite into this cool topic. Before we dive in, though, let’s first begin with a quick example.

More Than Color


Who knew that there was such a pattern to the usage of color! It makes sense that certain colors, like blue and red, tend to be more common. Psychologically, these colors tend to carry more meaning, and so it makes sense that book cover designers would use them a lot to have the best chance of grabbing your attention.

Aside from color, another important feature of book covers are the symbols used. How many times have you picked up a book and then immediately put it back down because you knew that it was going to be ‘another chick-lit’? Certain genres tend to show certain objects on their book covers, because of what these objects symbolize to us as the customers. Flowers are typically associated with love, and other positive emotions, while fire tends to be associated with darker themes like anger and rebellion. Applications to Real Life


Colors and symbols are only two of the features used on book covers in order to encourage you to buy a book. Your decisions on which books you’re attracted are being influenced before you even fully process what you are seeing. This is all pretty cool, but so what? Well, for one, as customers, you are now better equipped to understand your purchasing habits, and to analyze why you are drawn to certain books over others. And, if you find yourself needing to design book covers, either now or in the future, these features are important to think about. Consciously or not, people really do judge a book by a cover. Make yours stand out, because as they say, a cover is worth a thousand words.
